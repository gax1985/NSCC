[Law - Canada] CEA Section 31.1 - Burden of Proof;The person seeking to admit the evidence must prove its authenticity.
[Law - Canada] CEA Section 31.1 - The "Threshold";"Evidence capable of supporting a finding that the document is what it purports to be" (A low threshold).
[Law - Canada] CEA Section 30 - Business Records Rule;Records made in the "usual and ordinary course of business" are admissible (e.g., automated logs).
[Law - Canada] CEA Section 30(10) - The Investigation Trap;Records made *specifically* for an investigation are NOT admissible as business records.
[Law - Canada] CEA Section 29 - Financial Institutions;A copy of a bank record is admissible if verified by an affidavit (No need to seize bank servers).
[Law - Canada] CEA Section 7 - Expert Witness Limit;Max 5 experts per side (unless the judge grants leave).
[Law - Canada] CEA Section 8 - Handwriting Comparison;Disputed writing/signatures can be compared to genuine samples to prove authenticity.
