


Let's review the requirements for agents and researchers in Nova Scotia legislation regarding the protection of information and reporting of data breaches.

### Requirements for Agents

**Applicable Act**: **Insurance Act (Nova Scotia)**, Chapter 231 of the Acts of 1989.

**References**:

- **Insurance Act, c. 231, s. 52**: This section outlines the licensing requirements for insurance agents, including the need to complete specific courses and exams to ensure they understand the legal and ethical obligations related to handling personal information.
    

**Quotation from the Act**:

> **Section 52**: _An individual shall not be issued a life insurance license that restricts the licensee to act only as an accident and sickness insurance agent unless the individual has successfully completed the Life Licensing Qualification Program (LLQP) and passed the provincial licensing exam._

### Requirements for Researchers

**Applicable Act**: **Personal Health Information Act (PHIA)**, Chapter 41 of the Acts of 2010.

**References**:

- **PHIA, c. 41, s. 61**: This section requires custodians, including researchers, to adopt reasonable safeguards to protect personal health information.
    
- **PHIA, c. 41, s. 62**: This section mandates that custodians must ensure that electronic information systems maintain an audit trail of access to personal health information.
    
- **PHIA, c. 41, s. 69**: This section outlines the requirements for notification in the event of a data breach, including notifying affected individuals and the Review Officer (Privacy Commissioner).
    

**Quotation from the Act**:

> **Section 61**: _A custodian shall protect personal health information by adopting reasonable administrative, technical and physical safeguards that ensure the confidentiality, security, accuracy and integrity of the information._

> **Section 62**: _A custodian who uses an electronic information system to maintain personal health information shall ensure that the system maintains an audit trail of the following:_ 
> 
>>> (i) the identity of the person who accessed the information system,
>>> (ii) _the date and time of access,_ 
>>> (iii) _the information that was accessed;_
>

> **Section 69**: _Where personal health information is stolen, lost or subject to unauthorized access, use, disclosure, copying or modification, the custodian shall notify the individual to whom the information relates at the first reasonable opportunity if the custodian believes on a reasonable basis that the information has been or may be misused._



>[!Discussion]
>
>The custodian has the responsibility of the protection of health information through the adoption of technical , physical and administrative safeguards. The intention is to protect the confidentiality, integrity and security of the data assets. The custodian has to maintain a clear record of who accesses the information system, the date and time of their access, and specifying the date and time of access. They also bear the responsibility of informing individuals whose information get used, disclosed, copied, modified, or accessed without authorization at the first reasonable opportunity.
